Principals

In the 1970’s Dieter Rams asked himself, “What is good design?” in an effort to combat, in part “impenetrable confusion of forms.” As a contributor to the vast and expandinguniverse of digital experiences, I’ve asked the same question in search for the driving factors behind my approach.
Focused

A product must have a clear objective. Every component – no matter how small or large – must know what role it plays and how it supports the larger system.

Understandable

A product, and the elements within, must be self contained and perceptible. Usage and interaction should be easily discovered and feel natural.

Balanced

A product must be proportionate to its purpose. Elements – technical or otherwise –should work in harmony and bear the appropriate amount of weight.

Appropriate

A product must be suitable in form and function to it’s ecosystem. Usage should match environmental and behavioral expectations.

Adaptable

A product must be structured to adapt and evolve. Form and function should be structured to accept shifts in environment and behavior.

Of Integrity

A product must be principled in purpose and execution. Details should never be overlooked and proven methods of craft should never be overlooked
